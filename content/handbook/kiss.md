## Kissing: The Art Of The Most Underrated Skill

[image src="https://picsum.photos/seed/kissing-intro/900/520" alt="Couple kissing softly"]

Kissing is the most universal form of physical intimacy and, in long-term relationships, the most commonly neglected. Couples who maintain a vivid kissing life consistently report higher relationship satisfaction than those who don't — and the correlation is so strong that researchers regard frequency and quality of non-sexual kissing as a useful diagnostic for the overall health of a romantic partnership.

This is not because kissing is symbolic. It is because kissing is doing real neurochemical work: simultaneous release of oxytocin (bonding), dopamine (reward), and serotonin (mood regulation), combined with sensory input from one of the most densely innervated parts of the body. A good kiss is, quite literally, a small chemical event.

---

## Why It Goes Wrong

Most kissing problems are not technique problems. They are attention problems.

Couples who have kissed each other thousands of times tend to settle into a single default kiss — same pressure, same duration, same tongue movement — that requires no thought. The kiss becomes a transition, a greeting, a prelude. The quality of attention that made early kissing electric is gone, and what remains is a habit.

The first step in better kissing, almost always, is slowing down enough to actually notice what you are doing.

---

## The Fundamentals

**Start slowly.** A kiss that begins at full intensity has nowhere to go. A kiss that begins as the barest brush of lips, holds for a moment, and only gradually deepens, builds tension that the more aggressive version cannot.

**Soft lips.** Tense lips communicate, accurately, that the kisser is not really present. Relax the mouth. The lips should be soft enough that the partner can feel them shape against their own.

**Vary the pressure.** Alternating gentle and firmer pressure prevents monotony and keeps attention engaged.

**Breathe.** Through the nose, slowly. Holding your breath turns a kiss into a chore for both people.

## Using The Tongue Well

Tongue technique is where most kissers either over-do or under-do it. The principle: the tongue is a guest, not an invader.

- Start with the lips and only introduce the tongue once both mouths have softened into each other.
- Begin by touching the partner's lips with the tip of the tongue, lightly. Wait for them to respond.
- When tongues meet, do not push. Explore gently. The best tongue kissing has a quality of conversation — call and response, not monologue.
- Vary speed and depth. A slow, deep moment followed by a withdrawal to light lip contact creates a rhythm that a constant tongue presence destroys.

**The thing to avoid:** the rigid, fast, repetitive tongue motion sometimes uncharitably called *the washing machine*. It is the single most common complaint about partners' kissing technique.

## The Mouth Is Not Just Lips

Restricting kissing to mouth-on-mouth contact ignores most of what makes physical kissing erotic. Some of the most powerful kissing happens off the lips entirely.

- **The neck.** Slow, soft kisses moving from the jawline down the side of the neck and into the collarbone. The skin here is thinner and more sensitive than most areas of the body.
- **The earlobe.** A gentle suck or a warm breath. Many people find this unexpectedly intense.
- **The jaw.** A trail of soft kisses along the jaw, ending at the ear.
- **The shoulder.** A firm, lingering kiss on the shoulder during embrace.
- **The wrist and the inside of the elbow.** Rarely kissed, surprisingly sensitive.
- **The forehead.** Not erotic, but extraordinarily intimate. A forehead kiss in the right moment communicates love better than most sentences.

## Using Teeth

Teeth used badly are a disaster. Used well, they introduce an edge of urgency that elevates a kiss from sweet to electric.

The rule is *suggestion, not pressure*. A gentle bite on the lower lip — held for half a second, then released — communicates desire without causing discomfort. A soft graze of teeth along the side of the neck is one of the most effective and underused moves in kissing. Hard biting, except by explicit agreement, is almost always unwelcome.

---

## Building And Breaking Tension

The best kissing is structured. It has dynamics — moments of intensity, moments of pause.

A useful pattern: build slowly, deepen, then *pull back* — foreheads touching, breath shared, eyes meeting — and only then deepen again. The brief withdrawal compresses the tension and makes the next escalation much more powerful than uninterrupted intensity would have been.

In a long kissing session, this kind of structure makes the difference between a kiss that feels exciting at the start and dull by the middle, and one that maintains its charge for as long as the kissers want it to.

## Kissing Beyond Foreplay

Kissing tends to disappear during sex itself, especially in positions where it is awkward. This is a missed opportunity — sex with active, present kissing throughout is consistently rated as more intimate and more satisfying than sex without it.

Positions that make kissing easy (missionary, face-to-face on the side, cowgirl with a leaning forward) all benefit from this. In other positions, deliberately pausing for a kiss is a small interruption with a large effect.

---

## Re-Awakening Kissing In A Long Relationship

If kissing has faded in your relationship, the most effective single intervention is to make a deliberate point of kissing *without progressing to anything else.* Sit somewhere comfortable, set aside fifteen minutes, and kiss — without it being foreplay, without it being a transition to sex, without it leading anywhere.

This sounds artificial. It works almost immediately. The reason kissing has faded is usually that it has become functional, a prelude to other things. Removing the function — making it the entire purpose of the time — restores the quality of attention that made it good in the first place.

## Common Mistakes

- **Too much saliva.** Excess wetness is the second-most-common complaint after the washing-machine tongue. Swallow occasionally.
- **Bad breath.** Obvious but routinely ignored. A quick check or a mint before sex is basic care.
- **Closed-mouth pecks during sex.** Mechanical, distancing, easy to fix.
- **Rushing past it.** Kissing for ten seconds before reaching for clothing is a missed opportunity.
- **Stopping eye contact entirely.** Eyes closed is fine — most kissing happens with closed eyes — but a moment of eye contact mid-kiss, or just after, is one of the most intimate things two people can do.

---

## A Closing Note

Kissing is the form of physical intimacy with the highest return on attention. A small amount of deliberate practice — slower, more varied, more present — produces a disproportionate improvement in how connected a couple feels.

It is also the most portable. Unlike most other things in this handbook, kissing requires no privacy, no preparation, no time of day. It is available whenever two willing people are within arm's reach of each other, and using that availability well is one of the simplest pleasures a relationship offers.
