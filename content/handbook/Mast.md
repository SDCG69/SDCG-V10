## What is Masturbation?

Masturbation is the act of sexually stimulating yourself — your own body, your own rules, your own pleasure. It is one of the most natural, safe and universally human experiences there is. People of every age, gender, orientation and relationship status do it, and those who say otherwise are probably doing it in secret.

Call it solo play, self-pleasure, getting yourself off — whatever the name, masturbation is the original sex education. It is where you learn what you like, how you like it, and just how good your own body can make you feel. And that knowledge makes everything else better.

## Why Masturbate?

The benefits go well beyond the obvious. Regular masturbation reduces stress and anxiety, improves sleep, boosts mood, strengthens pelvic floor muscles and deepens your understanding of your own sexual response. It releases a cocktail of feel-good hormones — dopamine, endorphins, oxytocin — that leave you glowing long after the moment has passed.

Crucially, masturbation is a powerful act of sexual self-knowledge. As Kate Moyle, psychosexual therapist and LELO's Sex Expert, puts it: "It makes us feel more able to tell our partner what we want and need. And never underestimate the power of indulging in me-time to rev up your sex drive and help you learn how to have better sex. The more sex you have, including with yourself, the more sex you want."

For couples, masturbation is not a sign of dissatisfaction — it is a healthy, independent part of sexuality. Watching your partner pleasure themselves is one of the most intimate things you can share. Solo sessions keep desire alive, reduce performance pressure and give you insights into each other that no amount of conversation can fully replace.

There is no correct frequency. Some people do it daily, some weekly, some rarely. All of it is normal, provided it is not interfering with your daily life or your relationship. The goal is pleasure — not a quota.

## Masturbation with Toys

Sex toys are not a replacement for a partner. They are tools — instruments designed to expand what is possible in solo and partnered play. There is no shame, nothing unusual and nothing to feel guilty about in using them. They are sold openly, used widely and when chosen well, they are genuinely transformative.

## Masturbation and Relationships

Solo pleasure and partnered sex are not competitors. They are different expressions of the same healthy sexuality. Masturbating during a relationship is normal, common and nothing to feel guilty about.

If you feel comfortable doing so, masturbating in front of your partner — or watching them — is one of the most revealing forms of intimacy. You learn exactly how they touch themselves, what pressure they use, what rhythm they prefer. It is a shortcut to becoming a better lover, and it strips away pretence in a way that nothing else quite manages.

Mutual masturbation — touching yourselves simultaneously or touching each other — removes performance pressure, allows for connection without penetration and can be deeply satisfying in its own right.

The key is honest, ongoing communication. What feels good. What you are curious about. What you would like more of. Pleasure, solo or shared, grows in proportion to how openly it is discussed.
