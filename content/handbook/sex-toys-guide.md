## Sex Toys — The Complete Guide

[image src=“media/img/toys-header.png” alt=“Sex Toys Guide”]

The sex toy market has transformed over the last decade. What was once a poorly-lit corner of a joke shop is now a multi-billion pound industry producing beautifully designed, body-safe, technologically sophisticated products that genuinely enhance sex lives. If you haven’t explored it recently — or ever — this guide will walk you through everything worth knowing.

We’ve organised toys into the following sections:

1. **Women’s Toys** — vibrators, wand massagers, rabbit vibrators, clitoral suction toys, G-spot vibrators
1. **Men’s Toys** — masturbators, prostate massagers, penis rings, penis pumps
1. **Couples’ Toys** — wearable vibrators, cock rings with vibration, couples’ vibrators
1. **Remote Controlled & App-Connected Toys**
1. **Anal Toys** — butt plugs, anal beads, anal vibrators, prostate toys
1. **BDSM Toys** — restraints, blindfolds, paddles, crops, sensation toys, collars
1. **Other** — strap-ons, massage candles, ben wa balls, suction cup dildos

A note on **body safety:** always check that toys are made from body-safe materials — medical-grade silicone, ABS plastic, glass, or stainless steel. Avoid toys made from jelly rubber, PVC, or TPE/TPR if they are porous (porous materials harbour bacteria and cannot be properly sterilised). Reputable retailers like the three listed throughout this guide — **Lovehoney**, **Ann Summers** and **Bondara** — stock body-safe products.

## 5. Anal Toys

Anal play is one of the most popular categories in the industry — and one of the most misunderstood. All genders and orientations enjoy anal stimulation. The anus has a dense concentration of nerve endings, and for people with prostates, anal penetration provides the most direct route to prostate stimulation.

**Key rule for all anal toys:** Every toy intended for anal use must have a flared base or retrieval cord. The rectum can draw objects inward, and a toy without a flared base can become lodged internally — a medical emergency. Never use a toy anally that does not have a flared base.

## Toy Care & Safety

**Cleaning:** Clean all toys before and after every use. Non-motorised silicone, glass, and stainless steel toys can be boiled or run through a dishwasher (top shelf, no detergent) to fully sterilise. Motorised toys should be cleaned with a dedicated toy cleaner or mild soap and water — never submerge the motor.

**Storage:** Store toys separately (cloth bags work well) to prevent silicone toys from touching and potentially bonding. Keep away from direct sunlight.

**Materials:** If a toy has a strong chemical smell, discard it. Body-safe toys should have no strong odour. Jelly rubber and low-grade PVC toys are porous, cannot be sterilised, and should be replaced with body-safe alternatives.

**Batteries vs rechargeable:** Rechargeable motors are consistently more powerful and more economical long-term. They are worth the extra initial cost.
